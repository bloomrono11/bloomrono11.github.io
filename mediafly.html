Cover Letter

Why Mediafly

To the Mediafly Engineering Team,

Value calculators and TCO models are deceptively complex engineering problems. The business logic is dense, the data relationships are non-trivial, and the output has to be clear enough for a sales rep to use confidently in front of a buyer. That challenge sits right at the intersection of backend precision and frontend clarity — which is where I've spent most of my career.

Over 15+ years I've built full-stack systems where correctness in the data layer directly translates to outcomes people act on. At Synechron embedded within Citi, I own Java/Spring Boot services that calculate and communicate trade risk metrics to analysts under strict SLA requirements — financial calculation accuracy is non-negotiable. At Privacy4Cars I built the entire multi-tenant SaaS platform from scratch, leading a 7-person team through complex billing and reporting workflows that non-technical operations staff depend on daily.

On the frontend, my production stack is TypeScript and Angular — component-driven architecture, reactive data flows, and data-rich dashboards for business users. React is an adjustment I'd make quickly given the shared TypeScript foundation and component model.

The AI-assisted development requirement caught my eye because you framed it as a mindset, not just a tool — and that's exactly right. I use Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, and Devin daily across the full SDLC: architectural exploration, automated PR reviews via Delphyne at Citi, dependency management via Renovate, and test coverage acceleration. It's a genuine productivity multiplier and I treat it as a core engineering competency.

On observability — I have production experience with AppDynamics and the ELK Stack for distributed diagnostics, and I'm currently completing Datadog onboarding training through Citi as we migrate our services.

Mediafly's focus on helping sellers quantify and communicate impact is a problem that rewards engineers who think about what the data means to the person reading it. That's how I approach systems — and I'd enjoy building them with your team.
