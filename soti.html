Leadership Approach

How I lead

Situational Coaching

Blanchard-certified. I adapt coaching style — directing, coaching, supporting, delegating — based on each engineer's skill and motivation level, not a one-size-fits-all approach.

Agile Facilitation

Sprint planning, standups, retrospectives, and backlog refinement across time zones. I partner with ScrumMasters to remove systemic blockers, not to control the sprint.

Technical Credibility

I give engineers concrete, specific guidance because I've built the systems they're working on. Code reviews, architecture sessions, and pairing — not just process oversight.

Growing People

1:1s, individual development plans, onboarding runbooks, and knowledge-sharing sessions that reduce ramp-up time and build long-term team capability.

Best Practices Culture

CI/CD quality gates, code review standards, TDD, observability-first design — introduced as team norms, not just personal habits, so the whole org benefits.

Modern Tooling

AI-assisted development (Claude Code, Copilot, Devin), automated PR reviews via Delphyne, and dependency governance via Renovate — introduced at team level at Citi.

Cover Letter

Why SOTI

To the SOTI Engineering Team,

What draws me to this role isn't just the technical scope — it's the mandate to build the capability of the organization itself. Coaching engineers, improving how teams work, and creating conditions where great software gets built consistently is work I've done across multiple roles and find genuinely rewarding.

Over 15+ years in software engineering I've led teams as both a hands-on architect and a people manager. At Privacy4Cars I built and grew a 7-person engineering team from scratch — hiring, onboarding, mentoring, and running performance cycles while remaining technically accountable for the platform architecture. I applied Blanchard Situational Leadership to adapt my coaching style to each engineer's experience level, which accelerated ramp-up and delivery velocity significantly. At Edifecs as Sr. Software Engineering Manager I led cross-regional teams across the US and Russia — facilitating sprint ceremonies, removing blockers, partnering with the ScrumMaster, and coaching on Agile practices that meaningfully improved predictability.

At Synechron embedded at Citi, I continue mentoring junior and intermediate engineers — conducting code reviews, running knowledge-sharing sessions on Spring Boot and Kafka design, and contributing to onboarding runbooks. I completed Synechron's Manager Effectiveness Program covering team performance, engineering culture, and continuous improvement, and I've introduced a modern AI-assisted development toolchain at team level that has become a genuine productivity multiplier.

What I bring to a Software Development Manager role at SOTI is the combination of deep technical credibility with genuine coaching instinct. Engineers trust me because I've built the systems they're working on — I give concrete, specific guidance on architecture, testing, and best practices, not just process overhead. And I understand the distinction clearly: the ScrumMaster facilitates the sprint; my role is to remove systemic obstacles and develop people.

SOTI's commitment to going where nobody has gone before — and your two decades of continuous innovation in EMM — aligns with how I think about engineering culture: always learning, always improving, never satisfied with good enough.

I'm based in Mississauga and the hybrid model works perfectly. I'd welcome the conversation.
