Cover Letter

Why Speechify

To the Speechify Engineering Team,

I want to be upfront about one thing: my primary language is Java, not Kotlin. The JVM foundation is the same and the syntax gap is smaller than it looks — I've been deliberately ramping on Kotlin as the clear direction for modern JVM development. I'd rather say that clearly than pretend otherwise.

What I bring fully is the systems thinking and GCP depth this role needs. At Synechron embedded at Citi, I design and own backend services processing 400–600K trade transactions daily — architecting API abstractions for cross-team consumption, tuning JVM performance under strict SLA constraints, and applying end-to-end thinking to eliminate bottlenecks. I've built Kafka pipelines with exactly-once-safe guarantees, multi-threaded request handlers with pod resource isolation, and Hazelcast in-memory caching layers — all on GCP in production.

GCP specifically is deep for me. App Engine, Pub/Sub, Cloud Functions, Cloud SQL, BigQuery, Cloud Storage, Cloud Armor — I've built production systems on all of them. At Privacy4Cars I made the cloud architecture choices from scratch, selecting data stores based on access patterns, building event-driven ingestion pipelines with Pub/Sub dead-letter handling, and designing lightweight backend services that stayed simple as the platform scaled to 300+ customers.

The Core Experiences mandate — foundational services and SDKs that every product team depends on — is exactly the kind of high-leverage systems work I find most interesting. You can't optimize locally; you have to think end-to-end about contracts, reliability, and how your decisions ripple across the platform. That's how I naturally approach engineering.

On the startup pace — I've done seed-stage engineering twice. I know how to move fast, make pragmatic calls about what ships now versus what needs more time, and stay heads-down without needing structure around me. What Speechify is doing for audio information access is something I'd actually use every day — and that matters to me.
